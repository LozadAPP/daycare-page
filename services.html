Creative Expression

Art & Creativity

Our art program encourages children to explore their creativity through various mediums. Each week we introduce new projects that help develop fine motor skills while allowing for self-expression.

Activities include finger painting, clay modeling, collage making, and watercolor techniques. We focus on the process rather than the product, helping children build confidence in their creative abilities.

  • Daily art exploration sessions
  • Monthly themed art projects
  • Sensory art experiences
  • Outdoor art activities

Music & Movement

Our music program introduces children to rhythm, melody, and movement through interactive sessions. We use instruments, songs, and dance to promote physical coordination and auditory development.

Each day includes a music circle time where children sing along, play simple instruments, and move to different rhythms. We incorporate multicultural music to expose children to diverse sounds and traditions.

  • Daily music and movement sessions
  • Introduction to basic instruments
  • Rhythm and pattern recognition
  • Creative dance and expression

Science Exploration

Our science activities introduce basic concepts through hands-on experiments and nature exploration. Children learn to observe, predict, and discover the world around them in age-appropriate ways.

We focus on sensory experiences, simple experiments, and nature studies that spark curiosity. Activities change with the seasons to connect children with the natural world.

  • Nature walks and observations

  • Weather and seasonal activities

Outdoor Play

Our outdoor program provides daily opportunities for physical activity, social interaction, and nature exploration. We believe outdoor play is essential for healthy development.

Our playground is designed to challenge children at different developmental levels while ensuring safety. We incorporate structured games and free play to develop both motor skills and social abilities.

  • Daily outdoor play (weather permitting)
  • Climbing structures and balance beams

Childcare Options Information

Nannies and Babysitters

"The best and biggest benefit to hiring a nanny or babysitter is convenience," says Katie Bugbee, senior managing editor and parenting expert for Care.com, an international online service that connects families with care for children, seniors, pets and homes. "The nanny comes to your house. You don't have the juggling act of packing all the daily necessities and shuttling your child to daycare before work."

For parents in L.A. who might not be able to make the typical closing time of 5:30 or 6 p.m. at a daycare center, a nanny or babysitter could be the best choice.

Nannies, however, are the most expensive childcare option. According to Care.com, the average weekly cost of a full-time nanny is $475, compared with $127 for a family childcare center and $186 for a daycare center. But if you have more than one child, a nanny or sitter might make sense economically.

"Depending on where you live, a nanny for two children can often be less expensive than two kids in daycare," says Bugbee. "Our pay calculator shows an average hourly rate of $13 in the L.A. area. But check it out to see the going rate for a nanny in your specific ZIP code."

Choosing the Right Care

If you have decided that a nanny or babysitter is the best option for your family, Bugbee suggests writing out what you will need from your sitter. Here is her list of questions to consider:

  • How often will we need a sitter?
  • How flexible do her or his hours need to be?
  • Will we need the sitter to drive the kids anywhere?
  • What additional tasks might we want the nanny or sitter to take on?
  • What certifications (early-childhood education degree, CPR) would we like the sitter to have?
  • What level of energy does our family need?
  • How much experience should we require?

Tax Considerations

Bugbee advises parents not to overlook taxes, and to communicate with the new hire about them.

"If it seems like you could pay $1,900 or more to this sitter, you should make sure the sitter knows during the interview that taxes will be withheld from his or her pay from day one," says Bugbee. "So many sitters are simply unaware of what goes on behind the scenes when it comes to payroll and taxes, so they're unlikely to bring this subject up with the family."
